# Exercise and Workout Guidelines for Metabolic Health

Regular physical activity increases insulin sensitivity, reduces blood pressure, improves lipid profiles, and is a cornerstone of type 2 diabetes management and prevention.

### Aerobic Conditioning (Cardio)
* **Frequency**: Aim for 3 to 5 days per week, totaling at least **150 minutes of moderate-intensity** activity (brisk walking, cycling, swimming, rowing) or 75 minutes of vigorous activity.
* **Impact on Glucose**: Muscles burn glucose for energy during cardio, lowering circulating blood sugar levels for up to 24–48 hours post-workout by opening non-insulin-mediated glucose pathways.

### Resistance Training (Strength)
* **Frequency**: Perform full-body strength training **2 to 3 times per week** (focusing on major muscle groups).
* **Impact on Glucose**: Increasing skeletal muscle mass provides a larger "sink" for storing blood glucose as glycogen, reducing insulin resistance over the long term.

### Key Precautions
1. **Hydration**: Drink plenty of water before, during, and after workouts.
2. **Monitoring**: If diagnosed with diabetes, check blood glucose before strenuous workouts. Do not exercise if fasting glucose is extremely high (>250 mg/dL with ketones) or too low (<100 mg/dL without a carbohydrate snack).
3. **Consistency**: Avoid skipping exercise for more than two consecutive days.
