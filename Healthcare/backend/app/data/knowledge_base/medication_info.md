# General Medication Information and Reference Guide

This guide provides basic educational information about several common medications. It is intended for general reference and educational purposes only. It does not contain dosage instructions or treatment decisions, which must always be determined by a certified physician.

### Metformin
Metformin is an oral medication widely used to manage type 2 diabetes. It belongs to a class of drugs called biguanides. Metformin works primarily by decreasing the amount of glucose produced by the liver and improving the body's sensitivity to insulin, thereby helping the cells use glucose more effectively. It is typically prescribed alongside diet and exercise improvements.

### Lisinopril
Lisinopril is a medication commonly prescribed to treat high blood pressure (hypertension) and heart failure, and to improve survival after a heart attack. It is classified as an ACE (Angiotensin-Converting Enzyme) inhibitor. Lisinopril works by relaxing blood vessels, allowing blood to flow more smoothly and making it easier for the heart to pump blood throughout the body.

### Atorvastatin
Atorvastatin belongs to a group of medications known as statins (HMG-CoA reductase inhibitors). It is commonly prescribed to lower cholesterol and triglycerides in the blood, which helps prevent cardiovascular diseases, including heart attacks and strokes. Atorvastatin works by reducing the amount of cholesterol produced by the liver and increasing the liver's ability to clear LDL ("bad") cholesterol from the bloodstream.

*Disclaimer: Never adjust your medication regimen or stop taking prescribed medication without consulting your doctor. Always discuss side effects and drug interactions with your primary care provider.*
