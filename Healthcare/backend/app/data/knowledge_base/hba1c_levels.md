# HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin) Levels and Guidelines

The HbA1c test measures the average level of blood glucose (blood sugar) over the past 2 to 3 months. It represents the percentage of hemoglobin (a protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen) that is coated with sugar. 

### Why is HbA1c Important?
Unlike daily fasting blood sugar tests that capture glucose levels in a single moment, HbA1c provides a stable, long-term overview of glycemic control. This helps in diagnosing and monitoring:
* **Prediabetes**: An early warning sign that insulin resistance is developing.
* **Type 2 Diabetes**: Monitoring treatment effectiveness and target compliance.

### Reference Ranges
* **Normal**: Below 5.7%
* **Prediabetes**: 5.7% to 6.4% (indicates a higher risk of developing diabetes)
* **Diabetes**: 6.5% or higher on two separate tests.

### Management and Target Goals
For individuals diagnosed with diabetes, the American Diabetes Association (ADA) generally recommends keeping HbA1c levels **below 7.0%** to minimize the risk of microvascular and macrovascular complications (such as kidney disease, neuropathy, and stroke).
