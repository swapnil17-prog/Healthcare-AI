# Cholesterol Profiles and Cardiovascular Guidelines

Cholesterol is a lipid molecule produced by the liver and obtained through food. While the body needs cholesterol to build cells and synthesize hormones, excess cholesterol in the blood can form plaques in arterial walls, leading to atherosclerosis and cardiovascular disease.

### Key Lipid Panel Metrics
* **Total Cholesterol**: The overall count of cholesterol in your blood. Healthy target: **Under 200 mg/dL**.
* **LDL (Low-Density Lipoprotein)**: Known as "bad" cholesterol. It carries cholesterol from the liver to the body's tissues. High levels build plaques. Healthy target: **Under 100 mg/dL**.
* **HDL (High-Density Lipoprotein)**: Known as "good" cholesterol. It acts as a scavenger, carrying bad cholesterol back to the liver for disposal. Healthy target: **Above 40 mg/dL (men)** or **Above 50 mg/dL (women)**.
* **Triglycerides**: A type of fat stored in fat cells and used for energy. High levels indicate metabolic risk. Healthy target: **Under 150 mg/dL**.

### Managing Cholesterol
Cardiovascular guidelines recommend a heart-healthy diet low in saturated and trans fats, rich in soluble fiber (oats, beans), regular aerobic cardiovascular exercise, and weight management. For elevated risk, statin medications may be recommended by a physician.
